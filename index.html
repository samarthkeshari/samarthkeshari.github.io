Rise & Fall of COVID-19 in the US

Overview

The COVID-19 pandemic in the United States is part of the worldwide pandemic of coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19). More than 34.7 million confirmed cases have been reported since January 2020, resulting in more than 612,000 deaths. The following chart shows the timeline of important COVID-19 events in the US and the rise and fall of COVID-19 cases around those events.

  • Mar 2020: Cases "start to multiply" in the US. Experts focus on "flattening the curve" and many states enforce stay at home orders.
  • Jun 2020: After months of lockdown, efforts to reopening the economy leads to surge in cases and the "curve is not flattening".
  • Oct 2020: US witness the start of a massive "wave 2" and begins to break records for daily cases/deaths.
  • Feb 2021: Massive efforts to vaccinate people commences, starting with elderly, followed by young adults.
  • Apr 2021: Cases and Deaths begin to drop.
  • Jun 2021: US records lowest cases and deaths.
